# VISION

> Coding agents: read this before making any decision. It tells you why this product exists and what we will and won't build.

---

Every human will be accompanied by a personalized team of AI agents — agents that know their context, act on their behalf, and compound their capabilities over time.

Two consequences follow from this that define everything we build:

---

## 1. Organizations Will Be Reimagined

A single person with the right agents can operate as a full organization — HR, Sales, Marketing, Legal, Engineering. Coordination costs collapse. New forms of collaboration emerge beyond the startup vs. corporation axis: loosely bound communities, goal-driven networks, one-person departments.

**We are building a network of these super-powered individuals — where coordination between them creates a new kind of organization, more capable and more fluid than anything that exists today.**

---

## 2. Data Sovereignty Will Determine Who AI Serves

AI gets smarter with more personal context. The trap: most people will surrender that context to centralized platforms in exchange for capability. Over time those platforms hold the leverage, not the individual. AI ends up amplifying the powerful and surveilling everyone else.

**We believe the user holding the keys is not optional — it is the precondition for AI being good.**

This means:
- **Principle:** cloud is fine — convenience always wins — but cloud must be private by design; open source and interoperable, transparent data flows
- **Hard constraint:** no lock-in, no dark patterns, user data security is non-negotiable
- **Tension to navigate:** user control vs. usability for non-technical users — default toward control, but don't let perfect sovereignty kill a usable product

---

## So We Are Building...

A platform where anyone can create and direct their own team of AI agents — context accumulated on their terms, stored under their control, working in their interest.

The long-term goal is a network of these individuals: sovereign, capable, and able to collaborate in ways that were previously only possible inside large institutions.

The product succeeds when a user is more powerful *and* less dependent on us.
